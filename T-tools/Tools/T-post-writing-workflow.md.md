# Workflow: Professional Pop-Culture Post Creation

## Step 1: Content Analysis
- Scan the provided English article/link.
- Identify the "Tea" (The most juicy or relevant part for the Israeli audience).
- Extract 3 key takeaways that create value or start a conversation.

## Step 2: Language & Tone (The YMD Voice)
- Translate concepts into Hebrew . Any word in English change into fonetic Hebrew.
- Use Yarden’s signature wit: sharp, funny, and slightly obsessed with pop culture.
- Ensure the flow is coherent and professional, not a "google translate" output.

## Step 3: Social Connection (The Closer)
- Always end with a "Conversation Starter" question.
- Focus the question on personal connection (e.g., "Who are you going with?", "Does this happen to you too?").
- Aim to turn a digital post into a real-life connection point for the members.

## Step 4: Formatting
- Use bullet points for readability.
- Add relevant emojis (✨, 🎀, 📸, 🍿, 💄).
# Workflow: Cross-Source Sync & Trend Identification

## Step 1: Scanning & Grouping
- Scan the "Automated Content Sources" list in `B-brain/`.
- Identify topics appearing in 2 or more sources (e.g., a drama mentioned in TMZ and analyzed in Afterschool).
- Group related articles together to form a "Story Cluster."

## Step 2: Quality Filtering
- Prioritize stories that have both a "Juicy Factor" (Pop News) and a "Cultural Insight" (Analysis).
- Filter out news that doesn't fit the YMD community pillars (e.g., skip pure politics or finance).

## Step 3: Synthesis (The YMD Layer)
- Use the "Breaking News" from the tabloids (Variety/TMZ) as the hook.
- Use the "Cultural Context" from the newsletters (Afterschool/Dazed/Nieman Lab) to add depth and "Why it matters."
- Create ONE coherent Hebrew post that combines these two layers.

## Step 4: Final Output
- Save the synthesized draft to `0-output/` using the naming convention: `YYYY-MM-DD-Trend-Topic.md`.
