# Example: Product Manager at a SaaS Company

This is what filled-in C-core files look like for a product manager. Use this as inspiration when filling out your own files.

---

## project-brief.md (filled example)

```
# Project Brief

## Who We Are
I'm Daniel Levy, Senior Product Manager at CloudBase, a B2B SaaS platform for construction project management. We help construction companies track projects, manage budgets, and communicate with subcontractors, all in one platform. 200+ companies use us, mostly mid-size construction firms.

## What Problem We Solve
Construction companies still run on Excel spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and paper. Projects go over budget because no one has real-time visibility. Subcontractors miss deadlines because communication is scattered across 5 different channels. We give them one source of truth.

## What Makes Us Different
- Built by people who worked in construction (not Silicon Valley people guessing)
- Works offline on construction sites (most competitors don't)
- Hebrew-first interface (most tools are English-only, our users aren't fluent)
- Simple enough that a 55-year-old site manager can use it without training
```

## icp-profile.md (filled example)

```
# Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are
Operations managers and project managers at mid-size construction companies (50-500 employees). They manage 5-20 active projects simultaneously. Usually 35-55 years old, not very tech-savvy but desperate for a better system.

## Their Goals
- See all their projects in one dashboard without calling 10 people
- Catch budget overruns before they become disasters
- Reduce the time they spend on weekly status reports
- Look organized when the CEO asks "where do we stand?"

## Their Obstacles
- Their team resists new tools ("we've always done it this way")
- They've tried other software and the team stopped using it after 2 weeks
- They don't have time for a long onboarding process
- Their subcontractors won't download yet another app

## Where They Hang Out
- Israeli Construction Association events
- LinkedIn (construction and real estate groups)
- Industry WhatsApp groups
- Trade shows (Build Israel, Construction Expo)
```

## voice-dna.md (filled example)

```
# Voice DNA

## Brand Personality
Clear, structured, no-nonsense. I communicate like someone who respects people's time. I use data, not opinions. I write the way I'd want a stakeholder update written to me: bottom line first, details if you want them.

## What We Are
- Data-driven (show me the numbers, not the feelings)
- Structured (clear headers, bullet points, bottom line up front)
- User-focused (every decision starts with "what does the site manager need?")
- Practical (ship it, learn, iterate)

## What We're NOT
- We don't do corporate buzzword bingo ("synergize," "leverage," "paradigm shift")
- We don't write 10-page documents when 1 page will do
- We're not theoretical. Everything connects to a real user problem.
- We don't hide behind ambiguity. Clear yes/no/maybe with reasoning.

## Example Sentences in My Voice
- "Based on 3 weeks of user interviews, the #1 pain point is budget visibility, not scheduling."
- "Recommendation: ship the MVP without the reporting module. Here's why."
- "This will take 2 sprints. The risk is the API integration with their accounting software."
- "The data says yes. Two out of three user cohorts showed 40% higher retention with the new flow."

## Patterns
- Bottom line first, then supporting details
- Numbers over adjectives (not "significant improvement" but "40% improvement")
- Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph.
- Uses headers and bullet points aggressively
- Active voice ("we decided" not "it was decided")
- No em dashes. Ever.
```
