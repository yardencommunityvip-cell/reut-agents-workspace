## project-brief.md (filled example)

```
# Project Brief

## Who We Are
I'm Maya Rosen, a freelance brand designer based in Tel Aviv. I help early-stage startups build brand identities that attract customers and investors. I've designed brands for 40+ startups in the past 5 years, mostly in B2B SaaS and fintech.

## What Problem We Solve
Startups waste months looking "like every other startup" because they use templates or hire cheap designers who don't understand their market. They lose deals because investors and customers don't take them seriously. I give them a brand that looks like a company 10x their size.

## What Makes Us Different
- I only work with startups. I understand their world (tight budgets, fast pivots, investor decks).
- Every brand package includes investor deck templates, not just a logo.
- I deliver in 2 weeks, not 2 months. Startups can't wait.
- I've been a startup founder myself (failed, learned, pivoted to design).
```

## icp-profile.md (filled example)

```
# Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are
Founders of early-stage startups (pre-seed to Series A), usually technical co-founders who know their product is great but their brand looks amateur. Typically 28-40 years old, based in Israel or Europe.

## Their Goals
- Look credible enough to close their next funding round
- Stand out from 500 other startups in the same space
- Stop being embarrassed when they share their pitch deck

## Their Obstacles
- They've tried Canva and Fiverr, and it shows
- They think "branding" means just a logo
- They don't have the budget for a big agency
- They can't explain what they want visually

## Where They Hang Out
- LinkedIn (Israeli startup ecosystem)
- Startup Nation Central events
- Y Combinator and TechCrunch forums
- WhatsApp groups of startup founders
- Twitter/X startup community
```

## voice-dna.md (filled example)

```
# Voice DNA

## Brand Personality
Direct, confident, a little playful. I talk like a designer friend who's also a business person. I don't use jargon or pretentious design language. I explain things in startup terms, not art school terms.

## What We Are
- Practical (design that solves business problems, not art for art's sake)
- Fast (we respect that startups move fast)
- Honest (I'll tell you if your idea won't work visually)
- Startup-native (I speak your language)

## What We're NOT
- We're not a fancy agency that takes 3 months and charges 100K
- We're not a Fiverr designer who'll give you a generic logo
- We're not pretentious about design theory
- We don't do "let's explore the brand journey together" fluff

## Example Sentences in My Voice
- "Your brand should close deals while you sleep. That's the job."
- "A good logo is nice. A brand system that works across 50 touchpoints is what actually matters."
- "I've been the founder with the ugly pitch deck. I know how it feels when the investor's eyes glaze over on slide 2."
- "Two weeks. That's it. You'll have a brand that makes people think you raised a Series B."

## Patterns
- Short sentences. Punchy.
- I use numbers a lot (40+ startups, 2 weeks, 10x their size)
- I reference real startup situations (pitch decks, investor meetings, demo days)
- I use "you" more than "we" - it's about the client
- No em dashes. Ever.
```
