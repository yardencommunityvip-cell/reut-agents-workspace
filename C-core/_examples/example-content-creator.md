## project-brief.md (filled example)

```
# Project Brief

## Who We Are
I'm Noa Shapira. I write a newsletter called "Growth Notes" about marketing psychology for SaaS marketers. 4,500 subscribers and growing. I also post 3x/week on LinkedIn about the same topics. I'm a one-person operation: I research, write, and distribute everything myself.

## What Problem We Solve
SaaS marketers read the same recycled "growth hacking" advice everywhere. They copy what worked for Dropbox in 2012 and wonder why it doesn't work in 2026. I break down the psychology behind why marketing works, using current examples from companies they actually know.

## What Makes Us Different
- I don't teach tactics. I teach the thinking behind the tactics.
- Every issue includes a real teardown of a real campaign (not made-up examples)
- I write from experience: 8 years as Head of Marketing at 3 SaaS startups
- I share what failed, not just what worked. Most marketing newsletters only show wins.
```

## icp-profile.md (filled example)

```
# Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are
SaaS marketers (in-house), usually 2-7 years of experience. They're past the junior stage but haven't cracked the "think strategically" level yet. Typically 26-35 years old, work at startups or scale-ups, mostly B2B.

## Their Goals
- Move from executing tasks to thinking strategically about marketing
- Understand WHY their campaigns work (or don't), not just follow playbooks
- Build a personal brand on LinkedIn to advance their career
- Have fresh, smart takes to share in team meetings

## Their Obstacles
- Information overload: too many newsletters, podcasts, and "experts"
- Can't tell good advice from recycled nonsense
- Their manager wants "what HubSpot does" but they know that's wrong for their stage
- No time to read 20-minute articles. They need insights they can use in 5 minutes.

## Where They Hang Out
- LinkedIn (actively posting and commenting)
- Substack (reading marketing newsletters)
- Slack communities (Demand Curve, SaaStr, GrowthHackers)
- Twitter/X marketing community
- Marketing meetups and conferences
```

## voice-dna.md (filled example)

```
# Voice DNA

## Brand Personality
Curious, sharp, conversational. I write like I'm explaining something interesting to a smart friend over coffee. I'm opinionated but I back it up with evidence. I get excited about ideas and that energy shows in my writing.

## What We Are
- Curious (I genuinely find this stuff fascinating and it comes through)
- Evidence-based (I show the receipts, not just opinions)
- Conversational (you should hear my voice when you read this)
- Honest (I'll tell you when I'm wrong, when I don't know, and when everyone else is wrong)

## What We're NOT
- We're not a textbook. No academic jargon.
- We're not a hype machine. No "10x your growth overnight!"
- We're not generic. If you can swap our name with another newsletter and nothing changes, we failed.
- We don't talk down. Our readers are smart. We don't explain what a CTA is.

## Example Sentences in My Voice
- "Here's what's interesting about that Notion campaign. It's not the product-led growth everyone talks about. It's something sneakier."
- "I tested this on my own newsletter. Open rates went from 38% to 52%. Here's exactly what I changed."
- "Everyone says 'build in public.' Nobody talks about the week I lost 200 subscribers because I overshared."
- "Let's be honest: most A/B tests in marketing are statistically meaningless. You're reading tea leaves."

## Patterns
- Opens with a hook or observation, never a definition
- Uses "you" constantly. Makes the reader feel included.
- Short paragraphs. Rarely more than 3 sentences.
- Includes specific numbers and real examples
- Asks questions to the reader mid-text ("Sound familiar?")
- Uses "Let's be honest" or "Here's the thing" as transitions
- No em dashes. Ever.
```
