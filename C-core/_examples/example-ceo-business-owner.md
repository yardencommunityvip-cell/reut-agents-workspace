## project-brief.md (filled example)

```
# Project Brief

## Who We Are
I'm Yonatan Raz, CEO of BrightPath, a 35-person HR-tech company based in Tel Aviv. We build a platform that helps mid-size companies (100-1000 employees) run better performance reviews and grow their people. 80+ companies use us, mostly in tech and finance.

## What Problem We Solve
Performance reviews at most companies are painful theater. Managers hate writing them. Employees hate reading them. HR hates chasing everyone. And nothing actually changes. We turn it into a continuous feedback system that managers actually use, because it takes 3 minutes instead of 3 hours.

## What Makes Us Different
- We're built for the Israeli market first (Hebrew-native, fits local work culture)
- 3-minute weekly check-ins replace the annual review marathon
- Managers get AI-suggested talking points based on real team data
- We've been through the same growing pains as our customers (from 5 to 35 people in 2 years)
```

## icp-profile.md (filled example)

```
# Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are
VP HR or CEO at mid-size Israeli tech companies (100-500 employees). They've grown fast and their people processes haven't kept up. Usually 35-50 years old, pragmatic, allergic to HR buzzwords.

## Their Goals
- Keep their best people from leaving (retention is their #1 headache)
- Give managers a tool they'll actually use, not another system to ignore
- Show the board they have a "people strategy" without hiring a VP People
- Reduce the chaos of rapid growth without slowing things down

## Their Obstacles
- Their team is skeptical about "another HR tool"
- They've tried BambooHR or similar and adoption died in month 2
- The CEO is too busy to drive a culture initiative personally
- Their managers are engineers who became managers and struggle with feedback

## Where They Hang Out
- Israeli tech CEO WhatsApp groups
- Startup Nation conferences
- LinkedIn (Israeli tech ecosystem)
- CHRO roundtables
- YPO / EO local chapters
```

## voice-dna.md (filled example)

```
# Voice DNA

## Brand Personality
Direct, no-nonsense, founder energy. I write like a CEO who respects other CEOs' time. Short sentences. Clear asks. I share lessons from building our company because I believe in learning in public. Not polished corporate speak. Real talk from the trenches.

## What We Are
- Founder-honest (I share what actually happened, not the PR version)
- Action-oriented (every communication ends with a clear next step)
- Data-informed but gut-driven (numbers guide us, intuition decides)
- Respectful of people's time (if I can say it in 3 sentences, I don't write 10)

## What We're NOT
- We don't do corporate fluff ("leverage synergies," "align stakeholders")
- We're not academic or theoretical
- We don't hide behind ambiguity. Yes, no, or "I don't know yet" with a timeline.
- We're not salesy. We don't "reach out" or "circle back." We talk like humans.

## Example Sentences in My Voice
- "We lost 3 senior engineers in Q2. Not because of money. Because their managers didn't know how to have a real conversation."
- "Here's what I told the board: we're not ready for Series B until we fix retention. The numbers don't lie."
- "I spent 2 hours last week reading performance reviews our managers wrote. Half of them said nothing. That's the problem we're solving."
- "Decision: we're going with Option B. It's riskier but the upside is 3x. Here's the rollback plan if it fails."

## Patterns
- Bottom line first, then context
- Short paragraphs. One idea each.
- Uses specific numbers and real examples from our company
- Ends emails with a clear ask or next step
- Comfortable saying "I was wrong about X"
- Active voice. "We decided" not "it was decided."
- No em dashes. Ever.
```
